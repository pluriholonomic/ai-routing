## Referee B (empirical econometrician) — the empirics audit

**B-E1 Identification inventory, result by result.**
- *Tie atom vs grid nulls:* clean; two snapping rules; the null's
  conservative bias documented. PASS.
- *Author-anchor and lagged-copying:* the authors KILLED their own two
  headline identity claims with multiset-preserving hard nulls, and print
  the retractions. This is the strongest empirical hygiene in the paper.
  PASS (as negative results).
- *Species classification:* my main concern last round was selection-on-
  outcome. The split-sample design answers it: classification on days 1-6,
  outcomes on days 7-11; adopter level-persistence 89% vs 0% for every
  other cohort, repricing rates and the premium ladder replicating with the
  same ordering. This is textbook holdout validation and the 3.2/day OOS
  repricing of train-classified undercutters is about as far from an
  artifact as a two-week panel can show. PASS, with the boundary-parameter
  (0.05/day) sensitivity to be shown in the appendix as promised.
- *Randomized crossover:* n=752/arm exceeds the pre-registered 500 gate;
  the reversal history (flat at n=76 -> cheapest-firmest at n=752) is
  reported rather than laundered; first-position estimands consistent.
  PASS. Single-key caveat stands and is stated.
- *Steering audit:* position-0 selections only (uncontaminated); the
  eligibility-restricted version's n=1 critical cell is disclosed; the
  misclassification bounding argument is acceptable. PASS with the
  accruing-cells condition.
- *Hazard ladder:* in/out-of-sample split reported; only the gap channel
  claimed OOS. PASS as scoped.
- *Rationing inverse-ordering:* in-sample only (congestion panel not yet
  split). CONDITION: run the same train/test split before camera-ready.
- *Spread decomposition:* the below-cost grid is assumption-indexed and
  printed as a grid; the China/web3 tags are name-heuristics — CONDITION:
  move tag lists to the auditable registry with confidence flags, as done
  for capital tiers.
- *Staleness first pass:* n~28 age-known quotes. Correctly labeled
  directional. Do not promote without the panel-length version.
- *Multiple testing:* the test zoo is large, but every test in the paper
  traces to a dated pre-registration with ex-ante gates, sign-flip rules
  that were EXERCISED against the authors' own headlines twice, and
  nightly re-estimation. This is a better multiplicity regime than most
  published empirical IO. PASS.

# Referee Report — "Administered Menus and Focal Anchors" (v2)

*Same reviewer as v1. The authors' response is unusually direct: every major
point was either fixed with new analysis or the claim was demoted to match
its identification. Assessment of the revision:*

**M1 (retry identification): addressed honestly.** The new design (controls,
backward placebo with sign reversal, capacity-spillover IV) is exactly what
was asked. Notably, the IV result *weakens* the authors' original headline
and they restructured the paper rather than defending it — the asymmetric-
OLS-plus-tight-IV-bound presentation is the correct scientific statement.
The registered incident-instrument design is the right next step. Resolved.

**M2 (tie null): resolved and the paper is stronger for it.** The
independent grid-pricing null (13.4% vs 45.9% observed; 3.4x) converts the
paper's most striking fact from a possible artifact into a defensible
finding, and the 65/72 author-price levels result now carries the
coordination-relevant content. The unfired anchor-tracking test is
appropriately registered rather than claimed.

**M3 (entry conjecture): appropriately demoted.** The flat entry law
(0.16 vs 0.5, upward-biased against the finding) stands on its own as a
clean rejection of standard free-entry benchmarks in a market tailor-made
for them; the long-memory remark is now labeled as such with the horizon
problem stated. Fine.

**M4 (structure): resolved.** Three facts + one audit is a coherent paper.
The steering audit is, to this reviewer's knowledge, the first empirical
measurement of a platform's steering memory against the JRW design
criterion on any live marketplace, and its policy content (the rule taxes
undercutting) is publishable on its own.

**M5 (OOS): the authors report the collapse (0.90 -> 0.64) rather than
hiding it, scope the strategic rung as in-sample, and keep the gap channel
which does generalize.** This is how early-panel work should be written.

**M6 and minors: addressed;** the eligibility-restricted steering cells are
small (n=1 in the critical cell) and the authors say so plainly rather than
laundering it as robustness — acceptable given the misclassification
bounding argument, but the camera-ready should carry the accumulating-probe
version.

**Remaining weaknesses, stated for the record.** The dynamic results rest on
an 11-day high-frequency window (with a 3-year coarse backfill); several of
the paper's most interesting questions (anchor tracking, ABS pass-through,
the entry discriminator) are registered but unfired; and the welfare section
is now appropriately modest but correspondingly thin. None of these are
misrepresentations — the paper's claims now match its evidence, and the
nightly re-estimation infrastructure is a genuine strength for
reproducibility.

**Contribution assessment.** Novel market, novel purpose-built dataset,
three well-defended facts with the right nulls and sufficient statistics, a
genuinely new platform-audit statistic connected to current theory (JRW),
honest identification throughout, and full pre-registration with released
code and data. This clears the bar for a measurement-plus-audit paper at
this venue; the demoted results are correctly framed as an agenda.

**Recommendation: ACCEPT (minor revision).** Conditions for camera-ready:
(1) re-estimate all dynamic tables on the panel available at camera-ready
time and report both vintages; (2) include the accumulating
eligibility-restricted steering cells; (3) move the C1-C10 welfare apparatus
fully to the companion document and cite it.

Artifacts reviewed: the corrected LaTeX manuscript and rendered PDF, the H80

## Referee A — ACM EC / empirical IO

The correction is necessary and handled transparently. The original default
denominator pooled matched crossover requests with default-only monitoring on a
different model set. Restricting all policies to the 76 complete blocks gives
75/76 versus 61–64/76. The sign is nearly unchanged, but the correction removes
the false precision and, more importantly, separates the secondary all-position
screen from the carryover-robust first-position estimand.

The focal-anchor result remains the strongest contribution. In the refreshed
1,456-model-day panel, the observed-minus-cent-grid tie excess is 32.2 percentage
points with model-cluster 95% interval [25.8, 37.8]; the dime-grid excess is 18.0
points with interval [9.7, 25.1]. The administered-menu result is economically
interesting, but its dynamic hazard remains a short-panel finding.

The corrected H80 design is credible. Assignment replay is 100%, the earliest
balanced 500-per-arm prefix is fixed in code, pre-gate analysis can execute with
outcome columns absent, and continuation rows remain masked. However, the current
first-position counts are only 15, 20, 19, and 22. The paper therefore has two
promoted facts and one uncompleted experiment, not three facts.

**Recommendation: REVISE AND RESUBMIT.** I would convert to conditional accept
only after the preregistered first-position release and the earliest 30-day
pricing vintage. This is an accrual decision, not an invitation to specification
search.

## Referee B — operations research / platform systems

The dealer-and-dispatcher analogy, observability hierarchy, and agent/planner
problems remain useful. The corrected paper is stronger as a measurement paper
because it no longer treats default-only monitoring as a randomized control arm.
The gate artifact is also a meaningful systems contribution: it makes assignment
support, masking, cutoff selection, and release obligations machine-verifiable.

The present evidence does not yet identify the operational value of fallback.
The 76-block all-position gap is consistent with router substitution, but
treatment-specific carryover and one-account transport remain. Queueing and
capacity terms are still an organizing model rather than estimated primitives.

**Recommendation: WEAK REJECT / ENCOURAGE RESUBMISSION** for a top OR venue now;
**accept with minor revision** would be plausible if the 500-per-arm first-position
result retains the level and the paper keeps its current welfare boundaries.

## Meta-review

### Decision

**REVISE AND RESUBMIT — not accepted yet.** The manuscript is complete and the
core novelty remains plausible, but the v4 conditional accept cannot stand after
the denominator audit. The correction improves scientific validity while lowering
current causal readiness.

The remaining gates are now encoded and outcome-free:

- H80 first-position counts: 15 default, 20 cheapest, 19 second, 22 random, each
  targeting 500; 100% assignment replay. At the observed cadence, the pooled-rate
  forecast is about 459 hours and the slowest-arm forecast about 581 hours.
- Quote panel: 10 distinct dates observed, targeting the earliest 30-date prefix;
  20 additional observed dates remain. The original vintage is frozen to
  2026-07-07 through 2026-07-15.
- When both gates open, the release must contain Holm-adjusted first-position
  tests, the rank-gradient test, side-by-side nine-day and 30-day PM1/BM1/BM2/BM4
  estimates, an updated source manifest, the rendered paper, and a new review.

Any reversal of the default-firmness level or administered-menu sign reopens the
substantive review. Literal front-running, collusion, steering causality, and
global welfare remain outside the identified claims.
